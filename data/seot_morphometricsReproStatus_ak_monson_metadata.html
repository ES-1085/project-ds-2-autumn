Description:
Abstract:
This dataset includes morphometric measurements and pregnancy / dependency status from sea otters captured or collected (experimental harvests or recovered after the Exxon Valdez Oil Spill) in Alaska, 1947-2019 by the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) and the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (USFWS). Data collected include total body length, tail length, body mass, axillary girth, paw width, canine diameter and baculum length along with sex, age, and reproductive status of females (pregnancy / dependency status). For females with dependent pups, pup sex, mass, and total length are included when available. For harvested animals, data includes pregnancy status along with fetal sex, mass, and length.
Purpose:
These data were collected during experimental harvests primarily conducted in the 1960's and 1970's, or during sea otter captures related to USFWS and USGS sea otter sampling and tagging studies conducted from the late 1980's up to and including 2019, or recovered dead during the 1989 Exxon Valdez Oil spill (EVOS).
Supplemental_Information:
Morphometric data from experimental harvests and sea otter captures are appropriate for investigations of structural body size and condition in sea otters with some caveats: However, during experimental harvests (1967-1971), total length was measured as curvilinear length along the back as the otter lay on its stomach and occasionally along the curve of the back while the otters curled in a fetal position due to rigor mortis. In contrast length was measured as straight-line length as the otter lay on its back during early studies (1947-1963) and more recent live captures (1986-2019). Therefore, must use corrected “true standard lgth” when comparing lengths between methods. Paw width data were collected to quantify estimates of prey size based on paw width during sea otter foraging studies. Canine width was collected as a potential metric to sex skulls collected during carcasses collection survey. Reproductive status information was collected for studying sea otter reproductive biology but is useful as a covariate for body condition analysis. Baculum length was measured on males a way to estimate age-class of the animal.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
Individual otter IDs, collection dates, sampling locations, sex of individual, reproductive status of females and other values were cross-checked and verified with field worksheets. Morphometric data (mass, length, tail length, girth, paw width, canine width, baculum length) were recorded with varying degrees of accuracy. Mass was measured with a 100 lb spring scale periodically checked for accuracy by the Weights and Measures Section of the Alaska Department of Transportation and recoded to the nearest 0.5 lbs and then converted to kg. During experimental harvests (1967-1971), total body length (tip of the nose to the tip of the tail) was measured as curvilinear length along the otter's back as it lay on its stomach, but measured as straight-line length as they lay on their back during early studies (1947-1963) and during more recent studies (1986-2019), and recorded to the nearest 0.1-0.3 cm. Tail lengths were measure using a PVC pipe with a measuring tap glued along its length, which was slid over the tail and pressed firmly up to the base of the tail and recorded to the nearest 0.1-0.3 cm. Paw width was measured using a slide calipers and recorded to the nearest 0.1 cm. Canine width was also measured with a caliper and recorded to the nearest 0.1 mm. Baculum length was measure using a tape measure and feeling the ends of the bone and recorded to the nearest 0.5 cm. All morphometric measures had some amount of measurement error due to soft tissue positioning with curvilinear lengths, baculum lengths, girths, and paw widths the most difficult to measure with precision and accuracy while canine widths were the easiest to measure precisely and accurately. Dependent pup weights were measured with smaller spring scales (20-50 lb) and recorded to the nearest 0.1 lbs then converted kg. Newborn pups and fetuses were weight on ≤ 15-kg capacity balance during early studies (1947-1971). Age was primarily estimated by cementum analysis of a premolar tooth (+/- 1-2 yrs) and/or dental wear patterns. Generally, accuracy of ages estimates decrease with age. Animals aged as dependent pups are considered "known-age" and animals estimated to be >10 yrs are aged with the least amount of certainty.

Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
Geographic location data are reported at various levels of accuracy. We did not have harvest location for early studies (1947-1971) beyond the island name where it was collected so these are all reported as a single reference coordinate for each island where collections took place. Similarly, we did not have specific coordinates for animals collected during the 1989 Exxon Valdez Oil Spill response, so reference locations based on geographic names are reported. Most recent live-captured animals (1987-2019) have capture location recorded at the time of capture and are generally within 0.5 km of actual capture location.

Methodology:
Methodology_Type: Field
Methodology_Description:
Presented here are morphometric data from sea otters in coastal areas of Southeast, Southcentral, and Southwestern Alaska, since 1947. The measured otters were obtained in several ways, the majority from: 1) experimental harvests primarily conducted in the 1960's and 1970's, 2) sea otter captures related to USFWS and USGS sea otter sampling and tagging studies conducted from the late 1980's up to and including 2019, and 3) recovered dead during the 1989 Exxon Valdez Oil Spill response. The specific body measurements taken and their expected accuracy are detailed in the Data Quality - Attribute Accuracy section of this metadata record.
